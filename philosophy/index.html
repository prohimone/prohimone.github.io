Philosophy

The world is bigger than just America. Actually most of the people are living in the tropical regions and are still living with a very low level of technology. They are still looking for the basic needs of water and food and education and indeed also communication but also cultural values, and that they can feel secure about the education of their children that they can feel secure about the future.

So the whole urbanization that is taking place now and the technology in the cities that is developing there is not a very sustainable one. In the tropical regions we still have vast amounts of land, but we don't have jobs. So we need to look at more simple ecological sustainable systems that are going to be based basically upon what can sustainably be produced, based upon present-day sunshine. There are ways to provide energy from this tropical belt where you have all this rainfall, all these people, all this land and the right climate in terms of solar radiation. So the technology that is needed there is actually very very different from what we are discussing here so I feel indeed very far away from my home. There, we need technology that does not break down, which is simple, which is reliable. We need to look at systems that can function as an integrated unit.

Where on the one hand you can produce sugary juice from the sugar palms, but use that juice to produce ethanol for local fuels so people don't need to cut down the trees. that they can also yield electricity from that use that electricity to have a satellite link which can indeed be used for communication so that that village can not only have remote diagnostics for the mini factory but also can still sell their products at the right time at the right price and that they can also jointly buy products. In this way we maintain local communities, but on a low level of technology. so it's very interesting to see where this is all heading but we are looking at the major part of the world which is looking at very different technological issues.
